## SN-001 — Product Ladder Architecture Module
**Date:** June 26, 2026
**Source:** Top comment on Brian Moran (SamCart) YouTube video — "I Tried 134 AI Tools. Only 3 Will Make You Money." (https://youtu.be/TeqBPTNfgSI)
**Priority:** High — curriculum gap

### Observation
The top commenter on Moran's video noted: *"I always try to keep an eye out for SamCart offers because I know it converts, but more so when you have a product ladder (upsells, bumps, and down sales)."*

This surfaces a critical upstream gap in the current Beacon curriculum. The AI tools, the SamCart sales page skill, and the two-pass copy method all assume a product ladder already exists. Students who arrive without one will hit a wall: the tools demo beautifully but produce no revenue because the offer architecture underneath them is missing.

### Strategic Implication
Moran's forthcoming SamCart-native AI product will be optimized for entrepreneurs who already have a product ladder. It will never teach ladder design — SamCart has a commercial interest in users having *more* products to run through their platform. This is a gap Beacon can own cleanly.

### Proposed Module: "Build the Ladder First"
A dedicated module (or standalone lead magnet) covering product ladder architecture **before** any AI tool or sales page conversation begins. Suggested scope:

- **What a product ladder is and why it comes before the tech stack** — the commenter's insight reframed as a curriculum principle
- **The five rungs:** Lead magnet → Core offer → Order bump → Upsell → Downsell
- **Ladder design for digital product entrepreneurs** — pricing psychology, offer sequencing, value stacking
- **How the ladder unlocks the tools** — once the ladder exists, every Beacon tool (SamCart AI, two-pass copy, Signal Check, ACCESS course) has something to sell
- **Common ladder mistakes** — single-offer syndrome, mismatched price points, missing the bump

### Connection to Existing Beacon Assets
- The two-pass copy method skill should reference this module as a prerequisite
- The SamCart sales page workflow assumes a ladder — this module is the upstream dependency
- Could serve as a high-converting lead magnet in its own right (the irony of using a product ladder to teach product ladders is not lost)

### Next Action
Develop module outline and lesson structure. Assign to ACCESS course curriculum sprint when ready.
