This Cookie Policy explains how Beacon Momentum uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website and services. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them
  • First-party cookies: Cookies set by the website you are visiting
  • Third-party cookies: Cookies set by a domain other than the one you are visiting

2. How We Use Cookies

Beacon Momentum uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential functionality: To enable core features like user authentication and account access
  • Performance and analytics: To understand how visitors use our website and improve our services
  • Personalization: To remember your preferences and provide customized content
  • Marketing and advertising: To deliver relevant advertisements and measure campaign effectiveness
  • Security: To detect and prevent fraudulent activity and protect user accounts

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing cookies:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete cookies after each browsing session
  • Accept cookies from specific websites only

Note: Blocking all cookies may prevent you from using certain features of our website, such as logging in or accessing personalized content.

Learn how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Cookie Consent Tool

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer of our website.

5.3 Opt-Out Links

You can opt out of specific tracking technologies:

  • Google Analytics: Opt-out Add-on
  • Facebook Ads: Ad Preferences
  • Network Advertising Initiative: Opt-out Tool
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: Opt-out Tool

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want to be tracked. Currently, there is no industry standard for how to respond to DNT signals. At this time, our website does not respond to DNT signals, but we will continue to monitor developments in this area.

7. Mobile Devices

Mobile devices may use advertising identifiers instead of cookies. You can manage these identifiers through your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings > Privacy > Advertising > Limit Ad Tracking
  • Android: Settings > Google > Ads > Opt out of Ads Personalization

8. Cookies and GDPR Compliance

If you are located in the European Union (EU) or European Economic Area (EEA), we comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) regarding cookies. We will:

  • Obtain your consent before setting non-essential cookies
  • Provide clear information about the cookies we use
  • Allow you to withdraw consent at any time
  • Respect your right to object to certain types of tracking

9. Cookies and CCPA Compliance

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) gives you the right to opt out of the "sale" of your personal information. While we do not sell personal information in the traditional sense, some cookies may be considered a "sale" under CCPA.

You can opt out by:

  • Using our cookie consent tool to decline marketing cookies
  • Visiting our GDPR/CCPA Compliance page to submit a data request
  • Enabling the Global Privacy Control (GPC) in your browser
