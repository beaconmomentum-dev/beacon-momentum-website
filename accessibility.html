Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: November 29, 2025

Beacon Momentum is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all people, including those with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards to ensure we provide equal access to all users.

1. Our Commitment

Beacon Momentum strives to ensure that our website, programs, and services are accessible to people with disabilities. We believe that everyone deserves access to educational resources, mentorship, and community support, regardless of ability.

We are committed to:

  • Providing an inclusive and accessible digital experience
  • Continuously improving accessibility through user feedback and testing
  • Training our team on accessibility best practices
  • Working with accessibility experts to identify and address barriers
  • Ensuring that new features and content meet accessibility standards

2. Conformance Standards

Beacon Momentum aims to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at the AA level. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities and provide a better user experience for everyone.

We also strive to comply with:

  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Title III
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act (for U.S. federal accessibility requirements)
  • European Accessibility Act (EAA) (for EU users)
  • EN 301 549 (European standard for digital accessibility)

3. Accessibility Features

Our website and services include the following accessibility features:

3.1 Keyboard Navigation

  • All interactive elements can be accessed using a keyboard
  • Logical tab order throughout the site
  • Visible focus indicators for keyboard navigation
  • Skip navigation links to bypass repetitive content

3.2 Screen Reader Compatibility

  • Semantic HTML markup for proper content structure
  • ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) labels and landmarks
  • Alternative text for images and graphics
  • Descriptive link text (avoiding "click here" or "read more")
  • Proper heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3, etc.)

3.3 Visual Accessibility

  • Sufficient color contrast ratios (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
  • Text resizing up to 200% without loss of functionality
  • Responsive design that adapts to different screen sizes
  • No reliance on color alone to convey information
  • Clear, readable fonts with adequate spacing

3.4 Multimedia Accessibility

  • Captions for video content
  • Transcripts for audio content
  • Audio descriptions for important visual information in videos (where applicable)
  • Controls for pausing, stopping, and adjusting volume

3.5 Forms and Interactive Elements

  • Clear labels for all form fields
  • Error messages that are descriptive and helpful
  • Confirmation messages for successful actions
  • Sufficient time to complete forms (with options to extend time limits)

4. Assistive Technologies

Beacon Momentum is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen Readers: JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Screen Magnification Software: ZoomText, MAGic
  • Speech Recognition Software: Dragon NaturallySpeaking, Windows Speech Recognition
  • Alternative Input Devices: Switch controls, eye-tracking devices, adaptive keyboards

We test our services with a variety of assistive technologies to ensure compatibility and usability.

5. Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some areas of our website and services may not yet be fully accessible. We are aware of the following limitations and are actively working to address them:

  • Some third-party embedded content (e.g., videos, widgets) may not be fully accessible
  • Certain PDF documents may not be fully tagged for screen readers (we are working to remediate these)
  • Some older course materials may not meet current accessibility standards (we are updating these progressively)
  • Live video sessions may have limited accessibility features (we are exploring captioning solutions)

If you encounter an accessibility barrier, please let us know so we can prioritize improvements.

6. Third-Party Content

Beacon Momentum uses third-party services and integrations (e.g., payment processors, video platforms, CRM systems) that may have their own accessibility features and limitations. While we strive to choose accessible third-party tools, we do not have full control over their accessibility.

If you experience accessibility issues with third-party content, please contact us, and we will work with the provider to address the issue or provide an alternative.

7. Accessibility Testing and Audits

We conduct regular accessibility testing and audits to identify and address barriers. Our testing includes:

  • Automated Testing: Using tools like WAVE, Axe, and Lighthouse to scan for common accessibility issues
  • Manual Testing: Keyboard navigation, screen reader testing, and color contrast checks
  • User Testing: Gathering feedback from users with disabilities
  • Third-Party Audits: Engaging accessibility experts to conduct comprehensive audits

We use the results of these tests to continuously improve our accessibility.

8. Ongoing Improvements

Accessibility is an ongoing effort. We are committed to:

  • Regularly reviewing and updating our accessibility practices
  • Incorporating accessibility into our design and development processes
  • Training our team on accessibility best practices
  • Staying informed about emerging accessibility standards and technologies
  • Prioritizing accessibility in new features and content

9. Feedback and Assistance

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of Beacon Momentum. If you encounter an accessibility barrier, have suggestions for improvement, or need assistance accessing our content or services, please contact us:

  • A description of the problem
  • The assistive technology you were using (if applicable)
  • Your browser and operating system

We will respond to accessibility feedback within 3 business days and work to resolve issues as quickly as possible.

10. Alternative Formats

If you need content in an alternative format (e.g., large print, audio, Braille, accessible PDF), please contact us at accessibility@beaconmomentum.com. We will work with you to provide the content in a format that meets your needs.

11. Accommodation Requests

If you require accommodations to participate in our programs or services (e.g., sign language interpretation, extended time for assessments, alternative communication methods), please contact us at least 7 days in advance. We will work with you to provide reasonable accommodations.

12. Formal Complaints

If you believe that Beacon Momentum has not adequately addressed an accessibility concern, you may file a formal complaint:

  • Internal Complaint: Contact our accessibility coordinator at accessibility@beaconmomentum.com
  • External Complaint (U.S.): File a complaint with the U.S. Department of Justice, Civil Rights Division, at https://www.ada.gov/filing_complaint.htm
  • External Complaint (EU): Contact your national accessibility authority or enforcement body

13. Updates to This Statement

We review and update this Accessibility Statement regularly to reflect changes in our services, accessibility standards, and user feedback. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the statement was last revised.
